Steps to buying an electric car

Step 1

Buying an electric car is just like buying a normal car. Dealerships around the country have electric cars for sale like any other petrol or diesel car. While at the dealership, the dealer will take your details to check your eligibility for the Sustainable Energy Authority of Ireland (SEAI) grant and will look after all the administration. The purchaser will only have to pay the net cost of the car (i.e. sale price minus grant). Purchasers of new 100% electric cars will receive a grant of up to €5,000, while purchasers of plug-in hybrid electric cars will receive up to €2,500. For more details on the electric car grant scheme visit the SEAI website.

Step 2

Next step, your dealer will help you to fill out the online application form to register for a home charge point . In the following days, the ESB ecars team will contact you to confirm your home’s suitability for a home charge point. If the installation of the home charge point is not inline with the terms & conditions of the free installation. and requires non-standard work , the customer will incur a cost. Once you are approved for the SEAI grant and your home charge point application is successful, you can then finalise the order of your electric car with your dealer.

When you have purchased your ecar, an electrical contractor will contact you to organise the installation of the home charge point which is free for the first 2,000 new electric cars registered which qualify for the SEAI Grant. ESB does not install home charge points for those who do not qualify under the free programme, an independent supplier and installer will have to be contacted. As soon as your home charge point is installed, you will then receive your charge point access card from ESB ecars which will give you access to the nationwide network of public charge points. Also included in your pack are user instructions, guidelines and customer service helpline details.

At this stage you will be the proud owner of your new electric car! You will be able to charge your car at your home charge point and the public charge points around the country. Any queries, please email ecars@esb.ie. Alternatively, at each charge point there are helpline details should you need assistance. There are a number of ways to pay for charging your EV.

The cost of charging at home will simply be added to your regular domestic bill. If you opt to install a night time meter, you can avail of cheaper night rate electricity – please contact your energy supplier for details. You will need an access card in order to use any of the public charging infrastructure. Electricity at public and fast charge points will be at no cost to the user until the payment processing system is implemented and we will notify you of this in advance.

In the future a pre-paid system will be in operation. You will log into your account at www.esb.ie/ecars, choose your preferred energy supplier and tariff and pre-pay with your debit or credit card. You will then be able to check your balance and top up at any time online. Fast charge points are available throughout the country and can be located on the electric car charge point map. At the moment, similar to public charging, there is no payment system in place. Any queries, please email ecars@esb.ie. Alternatively, at each charge point there are helpline details should you need assistance.
